How Do You Start A Community Garden?

Community gardens are essential in every neighborhood! Here are 7 steps on how to start one:

  1. Find available land
    You will need to find a decent plot of land that is available, near water, and has decent soil or enough ground to build containers.
  2. Build engagement with your community
    Let the community know what you're doing! You will need a team to help you build your dream community garden.
  3. Find resources to support your garden
    Raise the funds. Most community gardens cost between $3,750 to $7,500 but there are grants and sponsorships available that can help you get started.
  4. Decide what to plant with the help of your neighbors
    You need to start thinking about the local climate, sunlight, and soil conditions. Which fruits and vegetables grow during which seasons? What produce would my community need the most?
  5. Build the site
    Decide how you will divide the site and if you prefer individual plots or individual beds and start building.
  6. Set out rules
    You will need a schedule. Who will water the gardens? How will you manage the weeds? Will the shifts rotate and when?
  7. Maintain and grow your garden!
    Keep the garden and the excitement of the community growing! Share in your success and your garden will flourish.
